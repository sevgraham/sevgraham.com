About Me

I earned my B.S. in Astronomy from the University of Arizona in 2024, with minors in Planetary Sciences, Physics, and Geosciences. I currently live in Tucson, Arizona, and my research centers on planetary dynamics and the orbital architecture of extrasolar and solar-system planetary systems.

As an undergraduate researcher at the Lunar and Planetary Laboratory, I studied the dynamical structure of the trans-Neptunian region using n-body simulations. This work was published in The Planetary Science Journal and presents a mechanism by which distant solar-system resonances lose strength.

Alongside my research, I work as a battery R&D technician at Ampcera, developing dry-process electrodes for solid-state cells. I am applying to PhD programs for Fall 2027, with research interests spanning exoplanet dynamics, n-body simulation, and solid-state battery technology.
